Startup Equity Lifecycle

Understanding how equity gets diluted and distributed as a company grows is fundamental to grasping venture capital.

Stage: Founding

The founder(s) start with 100% ownership of the company. This represents complete control and all future value.

What VCs Look For

Venture capitalists evaluate hundreds of pitches but invest in very few. Understanding their criteria is crucial for entrepreneurs and aspiring VCs alike.

Breaking Into Venture Capital

The VC industry is notoriously difficult to enter, but there are proven pathways for determined individuals.

Traditional Pathways

üéì Elite MBA Programs

Stanford, Harvard, Wharton MBAs often lead to associate roles at top-tier firms. Strong network effects and proven analytical skills make this the most common entry point.

üè¶ Investment Banking

2-3 years at Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, or similar provides financial modeling skills and deal experience that VCs highly value.

üíº Management Consulting

McKinsey, Bain, BCG experience demonstrates problem-solving skills and exposure to various industries and business models.

üöÄ Startup Experience

Operating experience at high-growth startups, especially in leadership roles, provides credibility when working with entrepreneurs.

Alternative Routes

Angel Investing: Start investing your own money to build track record and deal flow

VC Fellowships: Programs like Kauffman Fellows or Venture Capital Fellowship

Corporate VC: Intel Capital, Google Ventures, Salesforce Ventures offer easier entry

Emerging Managers: Join smaller, newer funds seeking talent and fresh perspectives

Investment Banking to VC: Lateral move after building financial expertise

Essential Skills to Develop

‚Ä¢ Deep industry expertise in high-growth sectors

‚Ä¢ Strong network within startup ecosystems

‚Ä¢ Pattern recognition for successful entrepreneurs
